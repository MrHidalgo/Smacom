Cooking technique

The technology sous-vide ("under vacuum") is a new absolutely unique method of cooking food. Food is placed in a vacuum-sealed plastic pouch and sealed. Then it is placed in hot water at accurately regulated temperature at around 47 to 70-80 degrees and the temperature is controlled until the food is done. The process itself takes several hours depending on a kind of product. The exclusion of air between the food and pouch allows retain all nutrients while the conventional cooking loses them in the process.

Essential features of cooking vegetables

The sous-vide allows cook vegetables salt-free while maintaining their shape and crisp texture. According to the sous-vide technique meat and fish cooked without seasonings and retain natural moisture. Ready meals are tender, soft, juicy and delicious at the end of cooking.

Temperature

The use of temperature much lower than for conventional cooking is an equally essential feature of sous-vide. Food cooked at temperature which is common for served meals. Food cooked sous-vide are soft, they heated evenly and slowly, the temperature is the same inside and on the surface of ready food. So the food doesn’t dry or burn.

Warm-up and storage method

Cut the pouch, place the food in a pot and then in a microwave oven or warm in a frying pan. Food cooked sous-vide can be taken in a travelling. It is thoroughly cooked, packaged and can be stored perfectly to 60 days at a temperature 0 to 6 degrees.
